Dissolved Oxygen (DO) — Most Critical Parameter
দ্রবীভূত অক্সিজেন (DO) — সর্বাধিক গুরুত্বপূর্ণ পরামিতি
కరిగిన ఆక్సిజన్ (DO) — అత్యంత కీలకమైన పరామితి

Target≥5 mg/L day · ≥4 mg/L dawn · >3 mg/L absolute minimum
Why Shrimp Suffer First at the Bottom
Shrimp are benthic animals — they live and feed at the pond floor where DO is always lowest. Surface DO of 5 mg/L can mean only 2–3 mg/L at the bottom. At DO <3 mg/L, shrimp cannot extract sufficient O₂ across gill membranes — they surface-gasp. Within hours: irreversible gill damage, immune collapse, Vibrio invasion. DO crashes kill within hours, not days. High salinity (30 ppt) + high temperature (30°C) further reduces maximum DO solubility by 20% compared to freshwater at 25°C.
⬇ Low DO (<4 mg/L) — Emergency Response
Shrimp crowd pond edges & surface at 2–5 AM. Paddlewheel running but shrimp still struggling. Feed tray empty next morning. Onset often follows algae bloom collapse or overcast nights. Monodon suffers first — deeper burrower, furthest from surface O₂.
  • Run ALL aerators immediately — do not wait for daylightDawn 2–6 AM is peak vulnerability window. Night watchman must check DO at 3 AM, not 6 AM.
  • Emergency water exchange from pre-treated reservoir 20–30%Monsoon canal water has low DO + pathogens. Never use untreated water during DO crisis.
  • Stop feeding completely until DO stable ≥5 mg/L for 2+ hours
  • CaO (quicklime) — suppresses organic O₂ demand 8–12 kg/acre
  • Zeolite — absorbs ammonia spike released during organic decomposition 15–20 kg/acre
  • Remove dead shrimp immediately — each 1 kg of decomposing shrimp consumes ≈ 1 g O₂/hr
⬆ Supersaturation (>12 mg/L) — Gas Bubble Disease
After dense algae bloom + intense sun. Nitrogen & O₂ gas emboli in haemolymph. Shrimp float with gas pockets visible under carapace. Exophthalmia (popped eyes) from retinal gas bubbles.
  • Reduce algae density — partial water exchange 15–20%
  • Stop fertiliser application. Cut feeding 50%.
  • Shade nets over nursery tanks during peak sun hours
⚠ DO–Algae Crash CycleDense algae bloom photosynthesises → DO 12–15 mg/L by 2 PM → algae respire at night → crash to 1–2 mg/L by 4 AM. 72–89% of feed phosphorus fuels this cycle. Monitor Secchi daily. <25 cm = crash within 24–48 hrs. Biofloc & probiotic management can break this cycle.

চিংড়ি কেন তলায় আগে কষ্ট পায়
চিংড়ি পুকুরের তলায় বাস করে ও খায় — সেখানে DO সর্বদা কম। উপরে ৫ মি.গ্রা/লি মানে তলায় মাত্র ২–৩ মি.গ্রা/লি। DO <৩-এ চিংড়ি ফুলকা দিয়ে পর্যাপ্ত O₂ নিতে পারে না — উপরে উঠে হাঁ করে। ঘণ্টার মধ্যে ফুলকার অপরিবর্তনীয় ক্ষতি, রোগ প্রতিরোধ শক্তি ভেঙ্গে পড়া ও ভিব্রিও সংক্রমণ শুরু হয়। DO সংকট দিনের মধ্যে নয় — ঘণ্টার মধ্যে মেরে ফেলে।
⬇ কম DO (<৪ মি.গ্রা/লি) — জরুরি পদক্ষেপ
চিংড়ি পুকুর পাড়ে ও উপরে ভোর ২–৫টায় ভিড় করে। প্যাডেল হুইল চলছে তবু চিংড়ি কষ্টে আছে। পরের দিন সকালে খাবার পাত্র খালি। বেশিরভাগ সময় শেওলার ক্র্যাশের পরে বা মেঘলা রাতে ঘটে।
  • সব এয়ারেটর তক্ষুনি চালু করুন — সকাল হওয়ার অপেক্ষা করবেন নাভোর ২–৬টা সর্বাধিক বিপজ্জনক। রাতের পাহারাদার ভোর ৩টায় DO মাপবে।
  • ভান্ডারের পরিষ্কার পানি দিয়ে জরুরি পানি বদল ২০–৩০%বর্ষার খালের পানিতে DO কম ও জীবাণু আছে। DO সংকটে কখনো পরিশোধহীন পানি দেবেন না।
  • DO স্থিতিশীলভাবে ≥৫ না হওয়া পর্যন্ত খাওয়ানো সম্পূর্ণ বন্ধ
  • CaO (পোড়া চুন) — জৈব O₂ চাহিদা দমন ৮–১২ কেজি/একর
  • জিওলাইট — পচনে নির্গত অ্যামোনিয়া শোষণ ১৫–২০ কেজি/একর
  • মরা চিংড়ি দ্রুত সরান — প্রতি কেজি পচা চিংড়ি প্রতি ঘণ্টায় ≈১ গ্রা O₂ নষ্ট করে
⬆ অতিরিক্ত O₂ (>১২) — গ্যাস বুদবুদ রোগ
ঘন শেওলা ব্লুম + তীব্র রোদের পরে ঘটে। রক্তে নাইট্রোজেন ও O₂ বুদবুদ জমে। চিংড়ি ভেসে থাকে। খোলের নিচে গ্যাস থলি দেখা যায়।
  • শেওলার ঘনত্ব কমান — আংশিক পানি বদল ১৫–২০%
  • সার দেওয়া বন্ধ। খাওয়ানো ৫০% কমান।
⚠ শেওলা-অক্সিজেন চক্রঘন শেওলা সালোকসংশ্লেষণ → বিকেল ২টায় DO ১২–১৫ → রাতে শেওলা শ্বাস নেয় → ভোর ৪টায় DO ১–২-তে পড়ে। খাবারের ৭২–৮৯% ফসফরাস এই চক্র চালায়। প্রতিদিন সেচি ডিস্কে স্বচ্ছতা মাপুন। <২৫ সেমি মানে ২৪–৪৮ ঘণ্টার মধ্যে ক্র্যাশ আসছে।

pH Management = Ammonia Management
At pH 7.5, only 0.3% of TAN is toxic NH₃. At pH 8.5 → 3%. At pH 9.0 → 15%. A pond with 0.5 mg/L TAN is safe at pH 7.5 but critically toxic at pH 9.0. Every algae bloom pushes pH up as CO₂ is consumed — simultaneously amplifying ammonia toxicity. Wide diurnal swings (pH 7.5 AM, 9.2 PM) cause chronic stress even when neither extreme is "critical" alone. Acid sulfate soils in coastal AP, Bengal delta make low pH a constant management challenge.
⬇ Low pH (<7.5) — Acid Pond
Common in acid sulfate soils, after heavy rain dilution, or post algae crash. H₂S fraction rises sharply below pH 7.0 (more toxic H₂S at lower pH). Shell calcification impaired — moulting fails. Plankton productivity drops. Metal toxicity (Al³⁺, Fe²⁺) from acidic soil leaching.
  • Agricultural lime Ca(OH)₂ — fastest correction 100–200 kg/acreApply at dusk. Check pH next morning. Raises pH 0.5–1.0 per application. Repeat in 48 hrs if needed.
  • Dolomite CaMg(CO₃)₂ — sustained buffering + Mg²⁺ supplement 100–200 kg/ha fortnightly
  • CaO — between crops, dry pond soil neutralisation 300–400 kg/acre
⬆ High pH (>9.0) — Algae Bloom Driven
Dense phytoplankton consumes CO₂ in photosynthesis → pH rises. NH₃ fraction jumps sharply. Shrimp irritable, erratic swimming. Water intensely green. Carbonate precipitation may cloud water white.
  • Alum Al₂(SO₄)₃ — high alkalinity + low Ca hardness 5–10 kg/acre
  • Gypsum CaSO₄ — lowers alkalinity, adds Ca²⁺ 50–75 kg/acre
  • Partial water exchange — dilute bloom 15–20%
  • Probiotic — competing bacteria stabilise bloom composition 1 kg/acre
↕ Wide Diurnal Swing (>0.5 units)
Root cause: dense phytoplankton + low alkalinity buffering capacity. Shrimp experience sub-lethal chronic stress throughout the cycle.
  • Dolomite — raise buffering capacity to 150–180 ppm 100–200 kg/ha fortnightly
  • Water exchange to dilute bloom density 10–15% daily × 3–5 days

pH ব্যবস্থাপনা = অ্যামোনিয়া ব্যবস্থাপনা
pH ৭.৫-এ মোট অ্যামোনিয়ার মাত্র ০.৩% বিষাক্ত NH₃। pH ৮.৫-এ → ৩%। pH ৯.০-এ → ১৫%। ০.৫ মি.গ্রা/লি TAN pH ৭.৫-এ নিরাপদ, কিন্তু pH ৯.০-এ মারাত্মক বিষাক্ত। শেওলার ব্লুম সালোকসংশ্লেষণে CO₂ গ্রহণ করে pH বাড়ায় — একই সময়ে অ্যামোনিয়া বিষাক্ততা বহুগুণ বেড়ে যায়। বাংলার ডেল্টা ও উপকূলীয় এলাকায় অ্যাসিড সালফেট মাটি কম pH-এর ঝুঁকি তৈরি করে।

How Ammonia Accumulates & Why pH Changes Everything
Ammonia is the primary metabolic waste from shrimp protein digestion — excreted via gills. Additional sources: decomposing feed, dead plankton, pond bottom sludge. Ammonia exists in two forms: ammonium ion (NH₄⁺ — harmless) and un-ionised ammonia (NH₃ — toxic). The proportion of toxic NH₃ increases dramatically with rising pH and temperature. At pH 9.0 and 32°C, up to 20% of TAN is NH₃ — meaning even "safe" TAN levels become lethal. NH₃ passes through gill membranes freely, causing tissue necrosis, blood O₂ carrying capacity reduction, and immune suppression — the perfect entry point for Vibrio septicaemia.
Elevated TAN (>0.1 mg/L) — Management
Reduced feeding (first sign). Gill reddening. Sluggish movement at pond edges. Increased susceptibility to bacterial infection. At chronic sub-lethal levels, FCR worsens significantly even without visible mortality.
  • Stop feeding — cut the primary nitrogen input immediately
  • Water exchange — fastest reduction 15–25%Clean reservoir only. Canal water during rain often has equal or higher ammonia from agricultural runoff.
  • Zeolite (clinoptilolite) — adsorbs NH₄⁺ from water column 15–20 kg/acreMost effective at salinity <5 ppt. Effectiveness drops above 15 ppt due to Na⁺ competition.
  • Molasses — carbon for heterotrophic bacteria to assimilate NH₄⁺ 5–10 kg/acre dissolvedC:N ratio approach. Works in 24–48 hrs. Also good for biofloc system management.
  • Probiotics (Bacillus spp.) — nitrification support 1 kg/acre
  • Maintain pH 7.5–8.0 — keeps toxic NH₃ fraction low even at elevated TAN
⚠ pH–Ammonia Danger MultiplierAt pH 8.5 and 30°C, ~5% of TAN is NH₃. At pH 9.0: ~15%. Any algae bloom-driven pH spike converts moderate TAN into an immediate lethal crisis. Managing pH is as important as reducing TAN itself.

Why Low-Salinity Ponds Face Extreme Nitrite Danger
Nitrite competes with chloride ions (Cl⁻) for uptake across shrimp gill membranes. Chloride in saltwater provides natural protection — it outcompetes NO₂ for gill transport proteins. At 20–30 ppt salinity, chloride concentrations are high enough to block most NO₂ uptake. But at 4–8 ppt (inland low-salinity vannamei ponds), Cl⁻ is scarce — even 0.3 mg/L NO₂ causes methaemoglobinaemia (blood loses O₂-carrying capacity). Monodon is even more sensitive — 0.1 mg/L can cause chronic stress at low salinity. Nitrite spikes commonly occur at DOC 20–40 as nitrifying bacteria (Nitrosomonas, Nitrobacter) establish.
Salinity-Specific Nitrite Risk
4–8 ppt:Cl:NO₂ may fall below 10:1 — extreme risk. Even 0.3 mg/L causes crisis. Add NaCl or KCl immediately.
8–15 ppt:High risk — monitor closely. Supplement chloride if NO₂ >0.3 mg/L.
15–30 ppt:Natural Cl⁻ provides buffer. NO₂ <0.5 mg/L usually manageable.
Nitrite Spike — Response Protocol
Pale/brown gills visible on dissection. Shrimp appear blue-grey (methaemoglobin). Feeding tray shows reduction. Peak risk: DOC 20–40 during biofilter establishment, after heavy rain dilution.
  • Emergency NaCl addition — raises Cl⁻ to outcompete NO₂ at gill 20–50 kg/acreInland low-salinity ponds ONLY. Fastest gill protection. Dissolve in water, broadcast evenly.
  • KCl (potassium chloride) — inland ponds where K⁺ also deficient 10–20 kg/acre
  • Water exchange 20–30%Most effective reduction method. Use clean reservoir water with low NO₂.
  • Zeolite — concurrent ammonia management 15 kg/acre
  • Probiotics — support nitrifying bacteria to complete the N-cycle faster 1 kg/acre
  • Stop overfeeding — reduce nitrogen input until spike resolved

H₂S — హైడ్రోజన్ సల్ఫైడ్ · నల్ల అడుగు చెరువు
Silent killer · Rotten egg smell · Monodon burrower — most at risk
নীরব ঘাতক · পচা ডিমের গন্ধ · মোনোডন গর্তকারী — সর্বাধিক ঝুঁকিতে
నిశ్శబ్ద హంతకుడు · కుళ్ళిన గుడ్ల వాసన · మోనోడన్ అడుగున బురద త్రవ్వేవి — అత్యధిక ముప్పు
Target0 — undetectable · <0.01 mg/L absolute maximum
How H₂S Forms & Why Monodon Suffers Most
Sulphate-reducing bacteria (Desulfovibrio) thrive in anaerobic conditions — dense organic sediment + low O₂ at pond bottom. They reduce sulphate (SO₄²⁻) to H₂S gas. H₂S solubility depends on pH: lower pH = more dissolved toxic H₂S (another reason to maintain pH >7.5). Black pond bottom + rotten egg smell = active H₂S production. P. monodon burrows into pond bottom sediment — directly exposed to highest H₂S concentrations. P. vannamei is less affected as it tends to stay in water column. At 0.1 ppm H₂S: equilibrium loss. At 4 ppm: instant death.
H₂S Buildup — Emergency Response
Pond bottom black and malodorous when disturbed. Feed trays empty — first warning (shrimp stop feeding before visible stress). Shrimp at surface in early morning. Pond surface may show dark patches. Monodon shows stress/mortality first; vannamei later.
  • Increase water exchange — most economical long-term solution 10–30% daily as DOC increasesPrevents organic accumulation that feeds sulphate-reducing bacteria. Use pre-treated water only.
  • Bottom aeration — re-oxygenate sediment interface Paddle aeration near edges. Do NOT violently disturb bottom during active H₂S — gas pulse release causes mass mortality.
  • CaO on pond bottom between crops 200–300 kg/ha dryRaises bottom pH, destroys sulphate-reducing bacteria. Most critical inter-crop treatment.
  • Zeolite — bottom detoxification 15–20 kg/acre
  • Probiotics (Bacillus subtilis/licheniformis) — organic sludge breakdown 1–2 kg/acre biweekly
  • Siphon bottom sludge carefully — slow, targeted, never sudden agitation
✖ Critical WarningNever drag nets violently through H₂S-active pond bottom. Sudden H₂S gas pulse release kills entire pond within 30–60 minutes. Siphoning must be slow and targeted. Harvest operations in H₂S-affected ponds must use paddle gentle movement only.

Targetsvannamei 15–25 ppt · monodon 15–30 ppt · rosenbergii 0–5 ppt
Osmoregulation & Isosmotic Point
Shrimp are osmoregulators — they maintain haemolymph (blood) ion concentrations different from surrounding water. This requires constant energy expenditure. P. vannamei's isosmotic point is approximately 25 ppt — at this salinity, osmotic work is minimal and growth is best. Below 15 ppt, shrimp must actively exclude excess water; above 30 ppt, they must actively retain water — both are energy-costly. Abrupt salinity changes disrupt osmotic balance rapidly — cells swell (low salinity) or shrink (high salinity). At 4–10 ppt inland ponds, ionic deficiency (K⁺, Mg²⁺) is as important as salinity itself — many inland mortality events are ionic deficiency crises, not salinity crises.
⬇ Sudden Salinity Drop — Monsoon / Freshwater Influx
Osmotic stress — shrimp cells swell as water floods in. Soft shells. Immunity crash. Vibrio opportunistic infection surge. Monodon collapses at <5 ppt (preferred: 15–30). Vannamei survives but growth severely impacted below 5 ppt.
  • Never add large freshwater volumes at once — max 3 ppt/day change
  • Sea salt or brine to restore salinity gradually ≤3 ppt increase per 24 hrs
  • KCl + MgSO₄ mineral supplement — restore ionic balance 5–10 kg/acre eachCritical for inland ponds. Low salinity doesn't just mean low NaCl — K⁺ and Mg²⁺ are proportionally even lower and must be individually supplemented.
  • Reduce feeding 50% during acclimation window
  • β-Glucan in feed — immune support during osmotic stress 0.1–0.2% feed
⬆ High Salinity (Summer Evaporation >30 ppt)
Shrimp thin and dehydrated (water leaves body). Growth stop. Feeding refusal. Moult intervals extend dramatically.
  • Gradual freshwater addition — max 3 ppt reduction/day
  • Increase water depth to reduce evaporation-driven concentration
  • Morning water exchange with cooler water from deeper reservoir

Why Alkalinity Matters for Both pH and Shell Formation
Alkalinity (primarily bicarbonate HCO₃⁻ and carbonate CO₃²⁻) is the water's capacity to resist pH change — the buffer system. Without adequate alkalinity (below 80 ppm), small CO₂ additions or removals cause dramatic pH swings. Shrimp also absorb calcium carbonate from water for shell/cuticle formation during moulting — this process directly consumes alkalinity. As culture period extends (higher biomass = more moulting), alkalinity is consumed faster. Alkalinity must rise with DOC or moulting failure becomes endemic.
⬇ Low Alkalinity (<80 ppm) — pH Instability + Moulting Failure
Wide pH swings (drops at night, spikes in afternoon). Soft shell that doesn't harden post-moult. Incomplete moults (shrimp trapped in old shell). Increased cannibalism of soft-shelled moulting shrimp. Chronic mortality especially at night/dawn moulting peaks.
  • Dolomite CaMg(CO₃)₂ — best balanced choice (Ca + Mg) 100–200 kg/ha every 2–3 daysUntil target range reached. Magnesium is also critical for neuromuscular coordination during moult.
  • Agricultural lime Ca(OH)₂ — faster acting 100–150 kg/acre
  • Sodium bicarbonate NaHCO₃ — gentle, no pH spike risk 5–10 kg/acre in intensive pondsBest option for sensitive DOC 5–15 juvenile period when lime-driven pH spikes can harm early PL.
⬆ Very High Alkalinity (>200 ppm)
CO₂ becomes limiting — plankton composition shifts, green water turns clear suddenly (crash risk). Carbonate precipitation creates white turbidity.
  • Gypsum CaSO₄ 50 kg/acre
  • Dilution with low-alkalinity water 10–15%

The Phosphorus-Plankton-Crash Cycle
72–89% of feed phosphorus is not absorbed by shrimp — it accumulates in water and sediment, fuelling continuous plankton growth. Dense algae blooms (Secchi <25 cm) create the classic DO boom-crash cycle. Algae crash releases massive ammonia from decomposing cells simultaneously depleting all O₂. The 24–48 hour window after a bloom crash is the most dangerous period in shrimp culture. Preferred plankton: Chaetoceros (diatom), Chlorella, Tetraselmis. Avoid: Cyanobacteria (blue-green, produces hepatotoxins), Dinoflagellates (red tide).
Dense Bloom (<25 cm) — Imminent Crash Prevention
  • Reduce feed 50% immediately — cut phosphorus input
  • Water exchange — dilute plankton density 15–25%
  • Probiotic (Bacillus) — competes with algae for nutrients 1 kg/acre
  • Do NOT apply algaecide to thick bloom — crash releases NH₃ + drops DO to zero
  • Run aerators 24/7 in anticipation of overnight crash
Plankton Crash (Water Suddenly Clear >50 cm) — Emergency
  • Emergency aeration — all aerators ON immediately
  • Stop feeding for 24–48 hours
  • Zeolite — absorb ammonia spike 15–20 kg/acre
  • Probiotic + molasses — restart bacterial nutrient cycling 1 kg + 5 kg/acre
  • Inoculate with healthy phytoplankton from reservoir within 48 hrs
⚠ Cyanobacteria (Blue-Green) AlertBlue-green bloom: dense surface scum, musty/earthy odour. Microcystis aeruginosa produces microcystin — liver toxin accumulates in shrimp hepatopancreas, reduces product export value. Do NOT apply CuSO₄ without species confirmation — killing bloom in situ crashes DO catastrophically. PCR/microscopy identification recommended before treatment.

Shrimp cluster at pond bottom; feeding stops at 22°C; metabolism slows; FCR worsens dramatically; immune function reduced at <24°C; Vibrio becomes more virulent at lower temperatures (certain cold-tolerant strains). Moult intervals extend 40–60% below 24°C.

  • Raise water depth to 1.5–1.8 m — thermal mass buffering
  • Vitamin C + E immune support in feed 1–2 g/kg feed continuously

  • Consider early harvest if temperatures expected <22°C for >2 weeks
  • Mineral supplement in feed — cold stress depletes mineral reserves 5 g/kg feed

High metabolic O₂ demand PLUS reduced DO solubility at high temperature → crisis. Vibrio growth rate doubles every 4°C above 25°C. Feeding frenzy → sudden lethargy. Shrimp at surface in afternoon. Feeding tray shows refuse accumulation.
  • Increase water exchange with cooler reservoir water 10–15%
  • Maximise aeration 10 AM–4 PM peak heat window

WSSV — হোয়াইট স্পট সিন্ড্রোম ভাইরাস
WSSV — తెల్ల మచ్చ సిండ్రోమ్ వైరస్
Viral · DNA Virus · dsDNA No Cure — Prevention Only Mass Mortality 3–10 Days
$90M
India annual loss
Patil et al. 2025
Agent: White Spot Syndrome Virus — largest shrimp virus (300 nm), infects 78+ crustacean species including crabs, crayfish, lobsters (wild reservoir hosts). Spread: contaminated water, infected broodstock, wild crustacean carriers (crabs carry virus asymptomatically), birds (mechanical vectors), shared equipment. Peak outbreak: rainy season — sudden temperature drop triggers virulence. India east coast prevalence: 6.48% (reduced from 25% — improved biosecurity awareness). WSSV replicates in nuclei of virtually all tissues — gills, hepatopancreas, lymphoid organ, haematopoietic tissue.
Field Identification
Day 1–2: Sudden dramatic drop in feeding (most sensitive early indicator — check trays twice daily). Day 2–4: Shrimp at pond edges, erratic swimming. White calcified spots 0.5–2.0 mm visible under carapace — press carapace, spots clear. Body turns pink-reddish (haemocytic infiltration). Loose, wrinkled shells. Empty gut visible through body wall. Day 5–10: Mass mortality 30–80%. Entire pond possible in 10 days.
Mandatory Prevention Protocol
  • SPF (Specific Pathogen Free) seed only — WSSV-PCR certified Hatchery cert required
  • Chlorinate ALL incoming water before pond filling 30 ppm TCCA · 24 hrs · neutralise before stocking
  • Bird netting over entire pond — herons & egrets are major mechanical WSSV vectors
  • Double 60-mesh filter screens on all inlet gates — exclude wild crabs (primary wild reservoir)
  • PCR test pond shrimp at DOC 15, 30, 45 using MPEDA/NACA approved labs
  • Pond dry + lime between crops CaO 300–400 kg/acre · 2 weeks dry
  • Avoid stocking during WSSV endemic months — check NSPAAD district data
Outbreak Management
  • No antiviral treatment exists — virus cannot be eliminated
  • Emergency partial harvest — recover marketable shrimp (>15 g) immediately
  • Stop feeding completely — reduce viral load amplification via stressed host
  • β-Glucan in feed — may slow progression if detected at DOC 15 early stage 0.2% of feed
  • Probiotic + Vitamin C — immune support to slow progressionCannot eliminate virus. Reduces secondary bacterial co-infection severity.
  • Notify MPEDA / State Fisheries Dept — legally required under AAHD Act
  • Disinfect ALL water before discharge TCCA 30 ppm · 24 hrs
  • Pond rest minimum 30 days + CaO treatment before restocking
✖ Biosecurity Non-NegotiablesNever use WSSV-positive pond water for other ponds even if "diluted." Never share nets, aerators, or feed equipment between positive and negative ponds without disinfection. Farm workers who handle positive ponds must change footwear before entering other pond areas. WSSV can survive 3–5 days in pond water. Zero-tolerance biosecurity is the only protection.

মাঠ পরিচিতি
দিন ১–২: খাবার হঠাৎ তীব্রভাবে কমে (সবচেয়ে সংবেদনশীল প্রাথমিক সংকেত)। দিন ২–৪: চিংড়ি পুকুর পাড়ে, অস্থির সাঁতার। খোলের নিচে সাদা স্পট ০.৫–২.০ মিমি দেখা যায়। শরীর গোলাপী-লালচে। ঢিলা খোল। খালি পেট। দিন ৫–১০: ৩০–৮০% মৃত্যু। ১০ দিনে পুরো পুকুর শেষ সম্ভব।

EHP — Enterocytozoon hepatopenaei

EHP — ఎంటెరోసైటోజూన్ హెపటోపెనాయి
Microsporidian Parasite · Fungal-like No Cure — Prevention Only
$571M
India #1 loss · 78% total
Patil et al. 2025
Agent: Enterocytozoon hepatopenaei — microsporidian parasite infecting hepatopancreatic tubule cells. EHP spores are extremely environmentally hardy — survive in pond soil for months, resistant to drying, UV, and most disinfectants. Spreads via spore-contaminated feed, water, and critically, through pond soil between crop cycles (which is why inter-crop drying + liming is non-negotiable). India east coast prevalence: 32.4%. More prevalent in vannamei than monodon. Co-infection with WSSV significantly worsens outcomes — combined loss 1.89 t/ha/crop.
Field Identification — The Key Sign
Most important diagnostic sign: extreme growth variation within the same pond by DOC 35–40. Shrimp of the same age showing 30–50% size variation — "giant" and "dwarf" shrimp coexist. Hepatopancreas pale, whitish, shrunken (HP must be compared against healthy shrimp to be useful). White/opaque stringy feces in feed trays (not pathognomonic — many causes). Reduced FCR. No mass die-off — chronic insidious productivity loss. Only PCR (hepatopancreas tissue) provides definitive diagnosis.
Prevention — The Only Effective Intervention
  • PCR test PL seed before stocking — reject EHP-positive lots absolutely NACA PL15 stage PCRThis is the most important single intervention. EHP-negative seed in EHP-contaminated pond = re-infection. Clean seed in clean pond = prevention success.
  • Aggressive inter-crop drying + CaO treatment — destroys spore reservoir 300 kg/acre · 2+ weeks full drySkipping this step guarantees re-infection. Spores in soil are the primary source of pond-to-pond transmission.
  • HDPE pond liner — eliminates soil spore reservoir permanently Investment pays back 2–3 crops
  • Chlorinate all incoming water 30 ppm TCCA · 48 hrs
  • Reduce stocking density — lower competitive stress 60–80 PL/m² max for EHP-endemic farms
Active EHP Pond Management
  • Organic acids (citric, fumaric, formic) in feed — acidify gut, inhibit spore germination 3–5 g/kg feed · 10–15 days
  • Probiotics — competitive exclusion in gut, reduce spore load 1 kg/acre biweekly
  • Water exchange — dilute spore concentration in water column 15–20% daily from DOC 40
  • Partial harvest by DOC 60 — if ABW deviation >25% between shrimp, cut losses
  • Do NOT feed ad libitum — overfeeding worsens hepatopancreatic damage

Agent: V. parahaemolyticus strains carrying pVA1 plasmid encoding PirA/PirB toxins (binary toxin). Toxins cause acute destruction of HP tubule epithelium — shrimp HP turns pale, white, mushy within hours of toxin production. Also: V. harveyi (luminescent vibriosis — infected shrimp/larvae glow green under UV at night), V. alginolyticus, V. harveyi. India east coast: V. parahaemolyticus 27.7% prevalence; V. alginolyticus 16.1%; V. harveyi 13.1%. Annual India economic loss: USD 40M.
Field Identification
AHPND (EMS): Mass mortality within first 20–30 DOC — up to 80% loss before any visible pond symptoms. Hepatopancreas pale, white, shrunken, mushy on dissection. Empty gut. Shrimp spiral-swim or rotate then sink and die — a hallmark behaviour. General Vibriosis: Shrimp/larvae glow green-blue at night (UV flashlight test) = V. harveyi luminescent strains. Opaque white muscles. Necrotic appendages. Black gills. Empty gut. Reduced feeding. Monodon baculovirus (MBV) co-infection common in monodon.
Prevention — Water Microbiome Management
  • TCCA water reservoir treatment 1 ppm · 24 hrs before use
  • BKC 80% (benzalkonium chloride) — routine Vibrio TVC control 500 mL/acre · monthly
  • Probiotics (Bacillus-dominant) — competitive exclusion in water & gut 1 kg/acre every 10 days
  • Organic acids in feed — gut acidification inhibits Vibrio colonisation 3–5 g/kg feed
  • Monitor Vibrio TVC weekly from DOC 30 Target <10³ CFU/mL
  • Avoid overfeeding — excess uneaten feed ferments to Vibrio-rich sludge
  • pH 7.8–8.2 — Vibrio growth optimal at 7.0–7.5, alkaline pond suppresses growth
Active Vibriosis Management
  • TCCA pond treatment 0.5 ppm · evening · all aerators ONDo not repeat within 5 days. Check DO next morning. Withdraw feed 24 hrs post-treatment.
  • BKC 80% 250 mL/acre · alternate with TCCA
  • Probiotic surge dose 2 kg/acre
  • Organic acid — gut pH correction 5 g/kg feed · 10 days
  • NO antibiotics without confirmed sensitivity testing + registered fisheries veterinarian prescription
✖ Antibiotic Resistance WarningMultidrug-resistant (MDR) V. parahaemolyticus strains dominate India east coast farms. Routine antibiotic use drives resistance further. Export consignments face rejection for antibiotic residues. India FSSAI and EU regulations prohibit treated shrimp exports. Use only with confirmed antibiogram from accredited laboratory.

RMS — Running Mortality Syndrome
RMS — রানিং মর্টালিটি সিন্ড্রোম
RMS — పరిగెత్తే మరణం సిండ్రోమ్
Multi-factorial · Not single pathogen Chronic Loss DOC 30–100
Cause: Not caused by a single pathogen — multifactorial. Combination of: poor seed quality (hatchery-stressed PL), high Vibrio load, shallow ponds (<1.2 m), high organic load, water quality stress, and opportunistic bacterial infections. India survey: production loss 1.1 t/ha/crop. Shrimp run to pond edges and die in small numbers daily throughout culture cycle.
Symptoms
Continuous daily mortality — 0.01–0.05% of stock per day dying on pond edges and morning scoop. No single dramatic event. Shrimp lethargic, dark gill coloration. FCR deteriorating week by week. Survival at harvest 40–60% (expected 70–80%). No definitive diagnostic sign — must rule out WSSV, EHP, AHPND by PCR before diagnosing RMS.
  • 24/7 intensive aeration — minimum 1 HP per 1000 kg biomass
  • Reduce stocking density — partial harvest if >DOC 50 Target <100/m²
  • Probiotic 10-day cycle throughout entire crop 1 kg/acre
  • Molasses when Vibrio TVC high 5 kg/acre dissolved
  • Daily water exchange DOC 40+ 10–15%
  • Vitamin C + immunostimulants continuously 1–2 g/kg feed
  • Increase depth ≥1.5 m — thermal & DO stability
কারণ: এককোনো জীবাণু নয় — বহুকারণীয়। দুর্বল বীজ মান, উচ্চ ভিব্রিও, অগভীর পুকুর (<১.২ মি), উচ্চ জৈব ভার ও পানি গুণমানের চাপের সংমিশ্রণ। ভারত জরিপ: ১.১ টন/হেক্টর/ফসল উৎপাদন ক্ষতি।

WFS — White Feces & Loose Shell Syndrome
WFS — সাদা মল ও ঢিলা খোল সিন্ড্রোম
WFS — తెల్ల మలం & వదులైన గుల్ల సిండ్రోమ్
EHP + Vibrio + Gregarine Gut & Shell Health Failure
Cause: White feces = EHP-damaged hepatopancreas + Vibrio gut infection + Gregarine (Nematopsis spp.) intestinal parasite + overfeeding + poor feed quality. Loose shell = mineral deficiency (Ca, Mg, alkalinity) + moulting disruption + EHP hepatopancreatic damage affecting cuticle biosynthesis. Both commonly occur together in stressed ponds.
Symptoms
White/pale stringy feces floating on pond surface and collecting in feed trays (most visible sign). HP pale, shrunken. Empty gut on dissection. Loose cuticle — shell separates from body, soft even days after moulting. Feeding tray shows reduced consumption. Slow growth despite apparent normal behaviour.
White Feces Protocol
  • Reduce feeding 30–50% immediately
  • Probiotic in feed + pond Pond: 2 kg/acre · Feed: 3 g/kg
  • Organic acids (citric/fumaric) in feed 3–5 g/kg · 15 days
  • Hepatoprotective supplement (liver tonic) 1–2 kg/ton feed
  • Water exchange — reduce Vibrio & organic load 15–20% daily
Loose Shell Protocol
  • Dolomite — Ca + Mg for shell biosynthesis 100 kg/acre
  • Ca + Mg mineral in feed 5 g/kg feed
  • Raise alkalinity to 150–180 ppm (moulting support)
  • Vitamin C + K for cuticle integrity 2 g/kg feed
কারণ: সাদা মল = EHP-ক্ষতিগ্রস্ত HP + ভিব্রিও অন্ত্র সংক্রমণ + গ্রেগারিন পরজীবী + অতিরিক্ত খাওয়ানো। ঢিলা খোল = খনিজ ঘাটতি (Ca, Mg, ক্ষারত্ব) + মোল্টিং ব্যাঘাত।

  • হেপাটোপ্রোটেক্টিভ সাপ্লিমেন্ট ১–২ কেজি/টন খাবার
  • পানি বদল ১৫–২০% প্রতিদিন

  • Ca + Mg খাবারে ৫ গ্রা/কেজি
  • ক্ষারত্ব ১৫০–১৮০ পিপিএম-এ বজায় রাখুন
కారణం: తెల్ల మలం = EHP-దెబ్బతిన్న HP + విబ్రియో పేగు సంక్రమణ + గ్రిగరైన్ పరాన్నజీవి + అతి ఆహారం. వదులైన గుల్ల = ఖనిజ లోపం (Ca, Mg, క్షారత) + వలువ విడవడం అంతరాయం.

Black Gill Disease — Gill Fouling
কালো ফুলকা রোগ — ফুলকা দূষণ
నల్ల గిల్ వ్యాధి — గిల్ కాలుష్యం
Zoothamnium · Epistylis · Protozoa Fusarium spp. Low DO + High Organic
Cause: Zoothamnium, Epistylis, Vorticella (ciliates) + Fusarium fungus + bacteria colonise gill surfaces under poor water quality conditions. These organisms are always present in pond water — they colonise gills only when host immunity is compromised by low DO, high organic load, or high H₂S. 22% of East India coastal farms showed darkened gills in suboptimal ponds.
Symptoms
Gills turn grey, brown, or black (normal: pale pink-white). Fluffy or slimy fouling material on gill surface under magnification. Shrimp at surface trying to increase O₂ uptake. FCR worsens despite adequate feeding. Reduced swimming vigour. Severe cases: haemocyte infiltration, gill necrosis — irreversible tissue loss.
  • Emergency aeration — all aerators ON Immediate
  • Water exchange — reduce organic load 20–25%
  • BKC 80% — protozoan + bacterial control 500 mL/acre · evening
  • TCCA 0.5 ppm · evening · next day alternate with BKC
  • Zeolite — TAN + H₂S bottom management 15 kg/acre
  • Dolomite — raise pH, suppress ciliate growth environment 100 kg/acre
PreventionFortnightly BKC + probiotic alternation dramatically reduces black gill incidence. Maintain DO >5 mg/L — ciliates explosively colonise gills when shrimp immunity drops from low DO stress. Healthy shrimp with strong immune function resist ciliate colonisation even in contaminated water.
কারণ: জুথ্যামনিয়াম, এপিস্টিলিস (সিলিয়েট) + ফিউসারিয়াম ছত্রাক + ব্যাকটেরিয়া দুর্বল জলমান পরিস্থিতিতে ফুলকা আক্রমণ করে। ২২% পূর্ব ভারত উপকূলীয় পুকুরে ফুলকা কালোকরণ দেখা গেছে।

ফুলকা ধূসর, বাদামী বা কালো (স্বাভাবিক: ফ্যাকাশে গোলাপী-সাদা)। চিংড়ি উপরে O₂ নিতে ভেসে থাকে। FCR খারাপ হতে থাকে।
  • সব এয়ারেটর তক্ষুনি চালু
  • পানি বদল ২০–২৫%
  • BKC 80% ৫০০ মিলি/একর · সন্ধ্যায়
  • TCCA ০.৫ পিপিএম · সন্ধ্যায়
  • জিওলাইট ১৫ কেজি/একর
  • ডলোমাইট ১০০ কেজি/একর
প্রতিরোধপক্ষকালে BKC + প্রোবায়োটিক পর্যায়ক্রমে দেওয়া কালো ফুলকার ঘটনা উল্লেখযোগ্যভাবে কমায়। DO >৫ বজায় রাখুন — কম DO-তে সিলিয়েট বিস্ফোরণমূলকভাবে ফুলকা আক্রমণ করে।
కారణం: జూథ్యాంమ్నియమ్, ఎపిస్టిలిస్ (సిలియేట్లు) + ఫ్యుసారియమ్ శిలీంధ్రం + బాక్టీరియా పేలవమైన నీటి నాణ్యత పరిస్థితులలో గిల్ పై పెరుగుతాయి. 22% తూర్పు భారత్ తీర చెరువులలో గిల్ చీకటిపడడం కనుగొనబడింది.
లక్షణాలు
గిల్స్ బూడిద, గోధుమ, లేదా నల్లగా (సాధారణం: లేత గులాబీ-తెలుపు). రొయ్యలు O₂ కోసం పైకి వస్తాయి. FCR క్రమంగా దెబ్బతింటుంది.
  • అన్ని ఏరేటర్లు వెంటనే ఆన్
  • నీటి మార్పు 20–25%
  • BKC 80% 500 మి.లీ/ఎకరా · సాయంత్రం
  • TCCA 0.5 ppm · సాయంత్రం
  • జియోలైట్ 15 కి.గ్రా/ఎకరా
  • డోలమైట్ 100 కి.గ్రా/ఎకరా
నివారణపక్షానికోసారి BKC + ప్రొబయోటిక్ పర్యాయంగా వేయడం నల్ల గిల్ సంభవాన్ని గణనీయంగా తగ్గిస్తుంది. DO >5 నిర్వహించండి — తక్కువ DO ఒత్తిడిలో సిలియేట్లు పేలుడుగా గిల్‌ను వలసపోతాయి.

White Gut, White Muscle & Muscle Cramp
সাদা পেট, সাদা পেশি ও পেশি টান
తెల్ల ప్రేగు, తెల్ల కండరం & కండరం తిమ్మిర్లు
Mg²⁺ · Zn · K⁺ Deficiency Osmotic / Temperature Stress
Cause: White gut = gut cleared of microbiome (starvation, Vibrio, EHP hepatopancreas failure). White muscle = magnesium and zinc deficiency + temperature stress (summer shallow ponds). Muscle cramp = osmotic shock, low Mg²⁺, low K⁺, abrupt salinity change (>5 ppt sudden), or bacterial muscle necrosis. Inland low-salinity (4–10 ppt) ponds at highest risk for mineral deficiency muscle conditions — K⁺ and Mg²⁺ are proportionally even lower than NaCl.
Symptoms
White Gut: midgut appears white/empty on dissection — no food, no microbiome, pale translucent. White Muscle: opaque white patches on abdominal segments (back) — shrimp partially bleached. Muscle Cramp: bent/curled body at tail segment — cannot swim straight, sinks to bottom. Can resemble net-stress cramp — rule out by reducing biomass before re-sampling.
  • Mg supplement (MgSO₄) in feed 3–5 g/kg feed · 10 days
  • Zinc + manganese mineral supplement 1–2 g/kg feed
  • KCl inland ponds — K⁺ restoration 10 kg/acre
  • Raise salinity slowly if <8 ppt — correct osmotic deficit ≤3 ppt/day
  • Reduce temperature stress — deepen water, shade net over nursery
  • Probiotic + prebiotic in feed — gut microbiome restoration 3–5 g/kg + 2 g/kg
  • Vitamin E + C — antioxidant muscle support 2 g/kg feed
কারণ: সাদা পেট = পেটের মাইক্রোবায়োম শূন্য (অনাহার, ভিব্রিও, EHP HP ব্যর্থতা)। সাদা পেশি = ম্যাগনেসিয়াম ও দস্তার ঘাটতি + তাপমাত্রার চাপ। পেশি টান = অসমোটিক শক, কম Mg²⁺, কম K⁺, হঠাৎ লবণাক্ততা পরিবর্তন।
লক্ষণ
সাদা পেট: মাঝের অন্ত্র সাদা/খালি। সাদা পেশি: পেটের অংশে ঘোলাটে সাদা দাগ। পেশি টান: লেজ ভেঙ্গে বাঁকানো শরীর — সোজা সাঁতার পারে না।
  • MgSO₄ খাবারে ৩–৫ গ্রা/কেজি · ১০ দিন
  • জিংক + ম্যাঙ্গানিজ খনিজ ১–২ গ্রা/কেজি
  • KCl — K⁺ পুনরুদ্ধার ১০ কেজি/একর
  • ধীরে লবণাক্ততা বাড়ান ≤৩ ppt/দিন
  • প্রোবায়োটিক + প্রিবায়োটিক ৩–৫ গ্রা + ২ গ্রা/কেজি খাবার
  • ভিটামিন E + C ২ গ্রা/কেজি খাবার
కారణం: తెల్ల ప్రేగు = ప్రేగు మైక్రోబయోమ్ ఖాళీ (ఆకలి, విబ్రియో, EHP HP విఫలం). తెల్ల కండరం = మాగ్నీషియం & జింక్ లోపం + ఉష్ణ ఒత్తిడి. కండర తిమ్మిర్లు = ఆస్మోటిక్ షాక్, తక్కువ Mg²⁺, K⁺, అకస్మాత్తు లవణీయత మార్పు.
లక్షణాలు
తెల్ల ప్రేగు: మధ్య ప్రేగు తెలుపు/ఖాళీ. తెల్ల కండరం: ఉదర విభాగాలపై అపారదర్శక తెల్లని మచ్చలు. కండర తిమ్మిర్లు: తోక భాగంలో వంగిన శరీరం — నేరుగా ఈదలేదు.
  • MgSO₄ ఆహారంలో 3–5 గ్రా/కి.గ్రా · 10 రోజులు
  • జింక్ + మాంగనీస్ ఖనిజం 1–2 గ్రా/కి.గ్రా
  • KCl — K⁺ పునరుద్ధరణ 10 కి.గ్రా/ఎకరా
  • లవణీయత నెమ్మదిగా పెంచండి ≤3 ppt/రోజు
  • ప్రొబయోటిక్ + ప్రీబయోటిక్ 3–5 గ్రా + 2 గ్రా/కి.గ్రా ఆహారం
  • విటమిన్ E + C 2 గ్రా/కి.గ్రా ఆహారం

Moulting Failure — Soft Shell & Incomplete Moult
মোল্টিং ব্যর্থতা — নরম খোল ও অসম্পূর্ণ মোল্টিং
వలువ విడవడం వైఫల్యం — మెత్తని గుల్ల & అసంపూర్ణ వలువ
Ca / Mg / Alkalinity Deficiency pH Swing + Stress Trigger
Cause: Moulting (ecdysis) requires calcium uptake from water + feed + alkalinity buffering for cuticle calcification. Below 80 ppm alkalinity, Ca uptake fails. Low Mg²⁺ impairs neuromuscular coordination during the moulting sequence. Abrupt pH, salinity, or temperature change during moult cycle causes fatal "locked moult" — shrimp cannot complete ecdysis, trapped between old and new cuticle. Cannibalism of soft-shelled shrimp escalates at high density. Moult synchrony with lunar cycle means problems cluster around new and full moon.
Symptoms
Soft, flexible shell that does not harden within 6–12 hours post-moult. Empty shells found with shrimp body still partially inside (fatal incomplete moult). Increased morning mortality at dawn — post-moult shrimp most vulnerable for 4–6 hours (cannot swim fast, easy prey). Cannibalism marks on soft-shelled shrimp. Reduced weight gain despite adequate feeding — repeated moult energy investment with poor calcification outcome.
  • Maintain alkalinity DOC-appropriate at all times — single most important step 120–180 ppm CaCO₃
  • Dolomite maintenance dose — fortnightly minimum 100 kg/ha
  • Ca + Mg + P mineral supplement in feed 5 g/kg feed
  • Vitamin C — chitin synthesis cofactor 2 g/kg feed
  • Stable salinity during moult windows — avoid changes >2 ppt
  • Night aeration maximum — post-moult shrimp require highest DO
  • Reduce stocking density — cannibalism on soft-shelled shrimp
Lunar Moult Synchrony TipShrimp in a pond moult on similar lunar cycles — clustering around new moon and full moon. Expect elevated vulnerability for 48–72 hrs around these periods. Do NOT apply disinfectants, aerator changes, or stressful sampling for 48 hrs around new/full moon. Maximise aeration, provide mineralised feed, and monitor closely.
কারণ: মোল্টিং-এ পানি ও খাবার থেকে ক্যালসিয়াম শোষণ + ক্ষারত্ব বাফারিং প্রয়োজন। ৮০ পিপিএম-এর নিচে ক্ষারত্বে Ca শোষণ ব্যর্থ। কম Mg²⁺ মোল্টিং সমন্বয় নষ্ট করে। হঠাৎ pH, লবণাক্ততা বা তাপমাত্রা পরিবর্তনে "আটকে যাওয়া মোল্ট" হয়।
লক্ষণ
মোল্টিংয়ের ৬–১২ ঘণ্টার মধ্যেও খোল শক্ত হয় না। চিংড়ির শরীর আংশিক ভিতরে রেখে খালি খোল পাওয়া যায়। ভোরে বেশি মৃত্যু। নরম চিংড়ির উপর নরখাদকতার দাগ।
  • সর্বদা DOC-অনুযায়ী ক্ষারত্ব বজায় রাখুন ১২০–১৮০ পিপিএম CaCO₃
  • ডলোমাইট রক্ষণাবেক্ষণ ১০০ কেজি/হেক্টর পক্ষকালে
  • Ca + Mg + P খনিজ খাবারে ৫ গ্রা/কেজি
  • ভিটামিন C ২ গ্রা/কেজি
  • মোল্টিং সময়ে লবণাক্ততা স্থিতিশীল রাখুন — >২ ppt পরিবর্তন এড়ান
  • রাতে সর্বোচ্চ বায়ু সঞ্চালন
চাঁদের সাথে মোল্টিং চক্রএকই পুকুরের চিংড়ি একই চান্দ্র চক্রে মোল্ট করে — অমাবস্যা ও পূর্ণিমার কাছাকাছি। এই সময়ে ৪৮–৭২ ঘণ্টা বিশেষ সতর্কতা প্রয়োজন। এই সময়ে জীবাণুনাশক বা চাপমূলক নমুনা নেওয়া এড়ান।
కారణం: వలువ విడవడానికి నీటి & ఆహారం నుండి కాల్షియం శోషణ + క్షారత బఫరింగ్ అవసరం. 80 ppm కంటే తక్కువ క్షారత వద్ద Ca శోషణ విఫలం. తక్కువ Mg²⁺ వలువ విడవడం నాడీకండర సమన్వయాన్ని దెబ్బతీస్తుంది. హఠాత్తు pH, లవణీయత లేదా ఉష్ణ మార్పు "లాక్డ్ మోల్ట్" కలిగిస్తుంది.
లక్షణాలు
వలువ విడిచిన 6–12 గంటల్లోనూ గుల్ల గట్టిపడదు. రొయ్య శరీరం పాక్షికంగా లోపల ఉంచి ఖాళీ గుల్ల కనుగొనబడుతుంది. తెల్లవారు ఎక్కువ మరణాలు. మెత్తని రొయ్యలపై నరమాంస భక్షణ గుర్తులు.
  • DOC ప్రకారం ఎల్లప్పుడూ క్షారత నిర్వహించండి 120–180 ppm CaCO₃
  • డోలమైట్ నిర్వహణ 100 కి.గ్రా/హెక్టార్ పక్షానికోసారి
  • Ca + Mg + P ఖనిజం ఆహారంలో 5 గ్రా/కి.గ్రా
  • విటమిన్ C 2 గ్రా/కి.గ్రా
  • వలువ విడవడం సమయంలో లవణీయత స్థిరంగా — >2 ppt మార్పు నివారించండి
  • రాత్రి గరిష్ట గాలి సంచలనం
చంద్ర వలువ విడవడం చక్రంఒకే చెరువు రొయ్యలు ఒకే చంద్ర చక్రంలో వలువ విడుస్తాయి — అమావాస్య & పౌర్ణమి సమయంలో. ఈ కాలానికి 48–72 గంటల ముందు నుండి జాగ్రత్తగా ఉండండి. జీవాణురహిత ఔషధాలు లేదా తీవ్రమైన నమూనా సేకరణ నివారించండి.
