Prescribing Safety

Drug Interactions &
Clinical Cautions

Review these interactions for all patients before prescribing ChronoShield Advanced. Critical interactions are non-negotiable; caution interactions require case-by-case assessment.

⚠ Critical
K2 MK-7 × Warfarin / VKAs
All Specialties
K2 MK-7 DIRECTLY interacts with warfarin and all vitamin K antagonists — INR will be affected. Mandatory disclosure to anticoagulation team, INR monitoring, and dose adjustment required. Safe with all NOACs (apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban).
⚠ Critical
ALA × Insulin / All Antidiabetics
Endocrinology · Cardiology · Nephrology
ALA potentiates ALL insulin and oral antidiabetics via AMPK/GLUT4. Mandatory blood glucose monitoring at initiation. Highest risk in fasted insulin-dependent patients. Never initiate without patient education on hypoglycaemia symptoms.
⚠ Critical
Resveratrol × CYP3A4 / CYP2C9 / CYP1A2
All Specialties — Mandatory Screen
Resveratrol inhibits CYP3A4 (>50%), CYP2C9, and CYP1A2. Affected drugs: immunosuppressants (tacrolimus, cyclosporin), antivirals (DAAs), statins, warfarin (CYP2C9), amiodarone, antiepileptics (phenytoin), some antidepressants. Drug level monitoring for narrow-window drugs.
⚠ Critical
Resveratrol × Pregnancy
Reproductive Health — Absolute Contraindication
NOT recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Insufficient human safety data; animal studies show potential developmental effects. Stop at least 1 month before planned conception and immediately upon confirmed pregnancy. Replace with dedicated prenatal supplement.
⚠ Critical
Resveratrol × Transplant Patients
Nephrology · Hepatology · Oncology
AVOID in transplant patients on calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus, cyclosporin). CYP3A4 inhibition may dangerously elevate immunosuppressant levels — risk of rejection if dose reduced without monitoring, or nephrotoxicity/CNS toxicity if levels rise. If prescribed, specialist drug level monitoring is mandatory.
⚠ Critical
Perioperative Safety Protocol
Post-Surgery — All Surgical Patients
STOP 7–14 days pre-op: Resveratrol, Tocotrienol, CoQ10 (antiplatelet risk). STOP 24–48 hrs: ALA (fasted hypoglycaemia — disclose to anaesthesiologist). SAFE pre/post-op: Vitamin C, Selenium, D3, K2 (if not on warfarin). RESUME Day 5–7 post-op once haemostasis confirmed.
⚡ Caution
Vitamin C × Advanced CKD
Nephrology — CKD 3b+
40 mg dose in this formula is safe for CKD stages 1–3a. In CKD 3b–5 and dialysis: Vitamin C metabolises to oxalate — accumulates in renal failure. Limit total Vitamin C ≤100 mg/day. Review with nephrologist in advanced CKD before continuing supplementation.
⚡ Caution
D3 × Granulomatous Disease
Immunology · Pulmonology · Dermatology
In sarcoidosis, TB, and granulomatous diseases, activated macrophages produce unregulated 1,25-OH D3 causing hypercalcaemia. Exogenous D3 can worsen this. Monitor serum calcium and 25-OH D3 before supplementing in patients with known or suspected granulomatous disease.
⚡ Caution
Fat-Soluble Vitamins × Advanced Cirrhosis
Hepatology — Child-Pugh B/C
Astaxanthin, Lycopene, Tocotrienol, CoQ10, D3, and K2 are fat-soluble and require hepatic metabolism for clearance. In Child-Pugh B/C cirrhosis, accumulation risk increases. Monitor levels and consider dose reduction in advanced cirrhosis. Discuss with hepatologist.
